# Basel III: Finalising post-crisis reforms

Source: [BIS official PDF](https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d424.pdf)

## What it is

The Basel Committee's finalisation of the Basel III framework. It complements the earlier Basel III reforms and focuses on making risk-weighted assets and capital ratios more robust, more comparable, and less variable across banks.

## Core points

- Revises the standardised approach for credit risk.
- Revises the internal ratings-based (IRB) approach for credit risk.
- Revises the credit valuation adjustment (CVA) framework.
- Revises the operational risk framework.
- Finalises the leverage ratio framework.
- Introduces an aggregate output floor for RWAs.

## Credit risk relevance

- Standardised approach uses standardised risk weights for exposures.
- IRB approach allows use of internal rating systems subject to supervisory approval.
- The framework aims to reduce excessive variability in risk-weighted assets.

## Output floor

- The output floor limits internal-model RWAs to no less than 72.5% of the RWAs produced by the standardised approaches.
- The publication shows a transition path ending at 72.5% on 1 January 2027.
