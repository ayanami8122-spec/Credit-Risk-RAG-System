# Guidance on credit risk and accounting for expected credit losses

Source: [BIS official page](https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d350.htm)

## What it is

Basel Committee supervisory guidance on sound credit risk practices for implementing and applying expected credit loss (ECL) accounting frameworks.

## Core points

- The move to ECL accounting was intended to address the weakness that credit loss recognition had been "too little, too late" during the financial crisis.
- The guidance complements accounting standards; it does not set Basel capital requirements.
- Timely identification and recognition of credit risk increases is essential for adequate allowances and capital adequacy.
- Risk management functions should be involved in ECL assessment and measurement.
